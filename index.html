What's my hair type?
Coily (Type 4A–4C): Super tight curls or zig-zag patterns that shrink up a lot. This hair is full of texture and volume.

Curly (Type 3A–3C): Defined curls that range from loose spirals to corkscrews. Curls bounce when pulled and usually have lots of volume and movement.

Wavy (Type 2A–2C): Has an “S” shape with some body and texture. Can be soft, loose, or a bit frizzy with more defined waves.

Straight (Type 1A–1C): Very straight (1A) to very slight bends or body (1C). This hair lies flat against the scalp.

You might be a mix of types - if you'd like a visual - a quick google search of "what's my hair type" will help!
What's my hair porosity?
Porosity is how well your hair absorbs and holds onto moisture. Try this quick test when you have a chance:

Grab a clean strand of hair (freshly washed, no products). Drop it in a glass of water.

Floats? You’ve got low porosity – your hair takes longer to absorb products.

Sinks slowly? That’s medium porosity – your hair takes in moisture at a balanced rate.

Sinks fast? That's high porosity – your hair absorbs moisture quickly but also loses it fast, so hydration is key.
